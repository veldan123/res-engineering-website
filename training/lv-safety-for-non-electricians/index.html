Low Voltage Electrical Safety for Non-Electricians

Practical half-day course for facilities staff, supervisors, and managers — covering what you can and cannot do safely near electrical systems, and when to call a Licensed Electrical Worker.

Course Overview

Many Singapore workplace incidents involving electricity happen not because of licensed electricians, but because non-electrical staff — facilities personnel, supervisors, or managers — took actions near electrical systems that they didn't realise were dangerous.

This course is not about training non-electricians to do electrical work. It is about giving them the knowledge to stay safe, understand their legal boundaries, recognise hazards, and know exactly when and how to get a Licensed Electrical Worker involved. It is one of the most practical safety investments a facility manager can make.

Common misconception: Many facilities staff believe that working on low-voltage systems (e.g., replacing a socket, resetting a circuit breaker) is safe to do without a LEW. Under Singapore's Electricity Act, most of these activities require a licensed person. This course clarifies exactly what the rules are.

What You Will Learn

  • Why low voltage is not "safe" voltage — how 230V causes electrocution
  • What electrical work is legally reserved for Licensed Electrical Workers in Singapore
  • What non-licensed persons CAN do legally and safely near electrical systems
  • How to identify common electrical hazards in commercial and industrial premises
  • Safe behaviour around switchrooms, distribution boards, and cable routes
  • How to safely manage a contractor or LEW carrying out electrical work on your premises
  • What to do when an electrical fault, trip, or emergency occurs
  • How to select and appoint a competent LEW contractor

Who Should Attend

  • Building and facilities managers responsible for electrical infrastructure
  • Operations supervisors who manage maintenance activities
  • Property managers and estate management personnel
  • HR and admin staff who are designated building safety coordinators
  • Any non-electrician who works in or manages a building with electrical infrastructure

Course Outline

Opening Session
Why electrical accidents happen to non-electricians. Singapore incident statistics. How Singapore's Electricity Act defines who can do what — explained in plain language.
Hazard Recognition
Identifying visible electrical hazards: damaged cables, overloaded sockets, switchboard issues, unauthorised modifications. When to act and when to call for help.
Safe Behaviour
Safe conduct in switchrooms and near live panels. How to safely accompany and supervise a contractor without putting yourself at risk. What to do in an electrical emergency.
Q&A and Assessment
Open Q&A — participants ask about real situations they face in their workplaces. Short assessment. Certificate of Completion issued on the day.
