Permit-to-Work (PTW) for Electrical Works

Comprehensive training on Permit-to-Work systems for electrical isolation — covering LOTO procedures, isolation verification, and safe re-energisation for supervisors and safety officers.

Course Overview

The Permit-to-Work (PTW) system is a critical safety control for electrical maintenance activities. When implemented correctly, it prevents electrical accidents by ensuring that electrical systems are properly isolated, locked out, and verified dead before any work begins.

This full-day course covers the complete PTW lifecycle — from hazard identification and isolation planning through to permit issuance, lockout/tagout (LOTO), and the safe re-energisation process after work is complete. It is aligned with Singapore's Workplace Safety and Health Act and the Electricity Regulations.

Mandatory for supervisors: Under the WSH (Electrical Work) Regulations, supervisors authorising electrical maintenance work must understand PTW requirements. Failure to implement a proper PTW system is a common cause of electrical fatalities in Singapore.

What You Will Learn

  • The purpose of the Permit-to-Work system and when it is legally required
  • How to identify all isolation points for a given electrical task
  • Correct procedures for electrical isolation, including HV and LV systems
  • Lockout/tagout (LOTO): principles, equipment, and application
  • How to verify "dead" using calibrated voltage detection equipment
  • PTW documentation: how to complete, issue, receive, and cancel permits
  • Safe re-energisation sequence and procedures
  • Common PTW failures and how to prevent them

Who Should Attend

  • Supervisors and engineers authorising or overseeing electrical maintenance
  • Safety officers responsible for electrical work safety procedures
  • Licensed Electrical Workers (LEW) and their assistants
  • Facilities managers who manage contractors performing electrical work
  • Any personnel who issue, receive, or are named on a Permit-to-Work

Course Outline

Morning — Theory
PTW systems overview. Singapore legal framework (WSH Act, Electricity Regulations). Hazard identification and isolation planning. LOTO principles and equipment.
Mid-Morning
PTW documentation workshop — completing permits correctly, common errors, and how they cause accidents. Case study review: real PTW failure incidents.
Afternoon — Practical
Practical scenarios: participants issue and receive PTW documents for simulated electrical tasks. Isolation verification exercise. Re-energisation sequence practice.
Assessment
Written assessment covering theory and scenario-based questions. Certificate of Completion issued to successful participants.
