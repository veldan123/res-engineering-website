Electrical Testing & Inspection Techniques

Hands-on technical training for engineers and technicians on performing electrical installation testing and commissioning to Singapore Standard SS 638.

Course Overview

Before any new or modified electrical installation in Singapore can be energised, it must be tested and inspected to confirm it meets the requirements of SS 638 — the Singapore Standard Code of Practice for Electrical Installations. This testing must be carried out or supervised by a Licensed Electrical Worker (LEW).

This full-day course gives engineers and technicians the practical skills to carry out the full suite of SS 638 tests correctly — using the right instruments, with the right technique, and producing a report that meets EMA and SP Group submission requirements.

Why this matters: Incorrect testing — even with the right equipment — is a common reason test reports are rejected by SP Group or fail EMA audit. This course focuses on getting it right first time.

Tests Covered in This Course

  • Insulation resistance testing — procedure, test voltages, pass criteria, and common failure causes
  • Continuity of protective conductors — measuring earth continuity from main earth to every outlet
  • Earth fault loop impedance (Zs) — test method, interpreting results, and verifying disconnection times
  • RCD / ELCB trip time testing — test currents, pass/fail criteria, 30mA and 100mA devices
  • Polarity testing — verifying correct live/neutral/earth connections throughout the installation
  • Prospective short-circuit current (PSCC) — measurement and comparison with circuit breaker ratings

Who Should Attend

  • Electrical engineers and technicians who carry out or assist with installation testing
  • LEW assistants developing their practical testing skills
  • Commissioning engineers new to Singapore's SS 638 requirements
  • Contractors whose test reports have previously been queried or rejected
  • Facility managers who want to understand what the testing process involves

Course Outline

Morning — Theory
SS 638 testing requirements overview. Test instruments — calibration, selection, and safe use. Test sequence and why the order matters. Documentation requirements.
Practical — Part 1
Hands-on insulation resistance and continuity testing on demonstration boards. Participants practice test setups, record results, and identify common faults.
Practical — Part 2
Earth fault loop impedance, RCD trip time, polarity, and PSCC testing. Participants work through each test type in sequence and record a complete test schedule.
Report Writing
Completing the Electrical Installation Test Report (EITR). Common rejection reasons from SP Group and how to avoid them. Q&A with our LEW engineers.
