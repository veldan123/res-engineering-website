Renewal of EMA Licences for Safe and Legal Electrical Installation

Operating electrical installations in Singapore without a valid EMA licence is illegal and dangerous. This guide walks you through everything you need to know about the renewal process — so your operations stay safe, legal, and uninterrupted.

Why EMA Licences Matter

In Singapore, the Energy Market Authority (EMA) regulates all electrical installations under the Electricity Act (Cap. 89A). Any premises with an electrical installation rated above 45kVA must have a licensed electrical installation, and that licence must be renewed periodically to remain valid.

Allowing your EMA licence to lapse — even unintentionally — can result in:

  • Legal liability under the Electricity Act
  • Your electricity supply being disconnected
  • Fines and enforcement action by EMA
  • Potential invalidation of your insurance coverage
  • Disruption to your business operations

⚡ Key Rule: All electrical installations with a maximum demand exceeding 45kVA in Singapore must have a valid Electrical Installation Licence (EIL) issued by EMA. This licence is linked to your appointed Licensed Electrical Worker (LEW).

Types of EMA Licences That Require Renewal

There are two main categories of licences that Singapore businesses need to manage:

1. Electrical Installation Licence (EIL)

This is the licence for the electrical installation at your premises. It must be renewed periodically, and the renewal is linked to your appointed LEW completing a periodic inspection and certification of your installation. The renewal period is typically every 1–5 years depending on the type and size of your installation.

2. Licensed Electrical Worker (LEW) Licence

Individual LEW engineers must also renew their personal EMA licences to continue practising. LEW licences in Singapore are typically valid for 3 years and must be renewed before expiry. If an LEW's licence lapses, they must stop all licensed work until it is renewed.

When to Renew: Key Deadlines

EMA sends renewal reminders to licence holders, but these can sometimes go to outdated email addresses or be missed during busy periods. As a best practice:

  • Check your EIL expiry date at least 3 months before it falls due
  • Arrange for your LEW to carry out the periodic inspection well in advance
  • Submit the renewal application to EMA before the expiry date — late submissions may require re-application
  • If your appointed LEW's personal licence is due for renewal, coordinate with them early

📅 Tip: Set a calendar reminder 4 months before your EIL expiry date. This gives you enough buffer to arrange the inspection, fix any defects found, and complete the submission before the deadline.

Documents Required for EIL Renewal

The exact documents may vary depending on your installation type and the changes made since the last inspection, but you will typically need:

  • Completed EMA renewal application form (submitted via the SP Services portal)
  • Valid Electrical Installation Test Report (completed by your LEW)
  • Updated single-line diagram (if any changes have been made to the installation)
  • LEW's valid licence copy
  • Applicable renewal fees (payable to EMA)

The Renewal Process Step by Step

Step 1: Appoint a Licensed Electrical Worker (LEW)

If you don't already have a LEW appointed for your premises, you'll need to appoint one. The LEW must hold a valid EMA licence for the category and maximum demand of your installation.

Step 2: Periodic Inspection

Your appointed LEW will conduct a thorough inspection of your electrical installation — checking for defects, safety hazards, compliance with current Singapore Standards (SS 638), and any changes that have been made since the last inspection.

Step 3: Rectification of Defects

Any defects or non-compliances identified during the inspection must be rectified before the renewal can be submitted. Your LEW will advise on the required works and can usually arrange for these to be carried out.

Step 4: Test Report and Certification

Once the installation is confirmed compliant, your LEW will complete the electrical installation test report and sign the certification. This is a legally binding document.

Step 5: Submit to EMA

The renewal application, together with the test report and supporting documents, is submitted to EMA through the SP Group portal. EMA will review the submission and issue the renewed EIL upon approval.

What Happens If Your Licence Lapses?

If your EIL expires and you continue operating your electrical installation, you are in breach of the Electricity Act. In practice:

  • EMA may issue a stop-work order
  • SP Group may disconnect your electricity supply pending regularisation
  • You may be subject to financial penalties
  • You may need to go through a full re-application rather than a renewal (more time-consuming and expensive)

If your licence has already lapsed, contact us immediately. In many cases, we can help regularise the situation as quickly as possible to minimise disruption to your operations.
